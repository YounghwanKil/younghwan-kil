# Values & Motivation
{: .no_toc }

This is the **core page for 자기소개서**. A cover letter lives or dies on an authentic
"why" — and that has to be in your own words. Below: (1) the value threads already
evidenced by your record, and (2) prompts to capture the parts only you can write.

## What the record already shows

Even before adding personal anecdotes, your body of work makes a consistent argument
about what you value:

1. **Trust as the real frontier of AI.** You didn't pick the most capable model or the
   flashiest benchmark — you picked *safety* and *reliability*. Every project answers
   "can we depend on this?" rather than "can it do more?".
2. **Do no collateral harm.** ASCG's whole design ethos is to suppress harm *only* where
   it appears and leave benign use untouched. That is a values statement encoded in a
   method: precision over blunt censorship.
3. **Honesty about uncertainty.** Evidential DL, conformal prediction, MC-Dropout — you
   repeatedly build models that admit what they don't know. In medicine especially, a
   model that defers is safer than a confident wrong one.
4. **Rigor and follow-through.** *Summa Cum Laude*, 5 international papers and 5 conference presentations, tens of thousands of
   evaluated samples. You finish things and you verify them.
5. **Giving knowledge back.** Four semesters tutoring physics; a wiki like this one.
   You care about making hard things understandable.

## Career direction (draft — refine in your voice)

- **Short term:** deepen generative-model safety and LLM alignment auditing during the
  M.S.; ship the LLM-safety papers currently under review.
- **Medium term:** work where trustworthy-AI research meets real deployment — a lab or
  team building safety/reliability into systems people actually use (healthcare AI,
  foundation-model safety, alignment).
- **The throughline:** be the researcher who makes powerful models *dependable* — safe,
  calibrated, honest about uncertainty.

## ✍️ Prompts — capture these in your own words

> Answer any of these in a sentence or two (Korean or English). Each becomes raw material
> for a 자소서 paragraph. Add answers directly under each prompt.

### Motivation
- **Why AI safety / trustworthy AI specifically?** Was there a moment — a model failing,
  an overconfident wrong prediction, a news story — that made this feel urgent?
- **Why the jump from industrial engineering to AI?** What did IE give you that you still
  use? What made you switch?
- **Why medical/clinical AI in particular?** Personal connection, or drawn to the stakes?

### Defining moments (STAR seeds)
- A **failure or setback** in research and what you changed afterward.
- A moment you **disagreed with an easier approach** and chose the harder, more rigorous
  one (ASCG's selective guidance is a candidate — what was the tempting shortcut?).
- A time your **teaching / helping others** changed how you understood something.

### Fit (fill per application)
- For *this company/lab*: which of your projects maps to their problem, and why you?
- What do you want to **learn** there that you can't get elsewhere?
- Where do you see yourself in **3–5 years**, and how does this role get you there?

### Personal
- Three words a labmate/PI would use to describe you — and a moment that proves each.
- Something outside research that says something true about how you work or think.
